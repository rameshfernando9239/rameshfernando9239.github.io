I'm Ramesh Fernando, a Senior Research Engineer operating at the intersection of advanced Digital Verification and specialized hardware architecture. My background spans over six years of rigorous industry experience—building robust UVM testbenches and driving silicon success—coupled with targeted academic research in VLSI design.

My work is driven by the challenge of optimizing Power-Performance-Area (PPA) for modern, compute-heavy AI workloads. I specialize in the microarchitecture design of runtime-configurable MAC units and systolic arrays tailored specifically for transformer-based Large Language Model (LLM) inference. By bridging the gap between theoretical hardware concepts and practical RTL implementation, I focus on pushing the boundaries of what is possible in Edge AI computing.
